DAY 8
Show Don't Tell & Climax

üé≠Show Don't Tell

Instead of telling readers what's happening, show them through actions, dialogue, and details!

Tell vs. Show Examples

Tell: Sarah was scared.

Show: Sarah's hands shook as she reached for the door handle, and she could hear her heart pounding in her ears.


Tell: The dragon was big.

Show: The dragon's shadow covered the entire village, and when it roared, windows shattered three streets away.

DAY 9
Falling Action & Resolution

üìâUnderstanding Falling Action

After the climax comes the falling action - events that happen as a result of the climax. Think of it as the "cooling down" period of your story.

What Happens in Falling Action?

  • Immediate consequences of the climax
  • Character reactions to what just happened
  • Cleanup or dealing with the aftermath
  • Smaller problems being solved
  • Characters processing their experiences
